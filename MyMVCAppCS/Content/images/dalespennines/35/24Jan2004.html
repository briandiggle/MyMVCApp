Ingleborough

24 January 2004
From Horton-in-Ribblesdale

473m ascent 16km

With Ross Bromley

We almost caused the 'the horton riots' by parking in the station car park. We were legally allowed to do so, but had to cross a small private road to get to it! First a station worker and then a local gent gave us a telling off. I was chuffed to see how my "gentle answer turned away wrath" with the local gent who wished us a good day on the fells as we left.

When I'm walking with Ross there is no need to pay any attention to the map. He is equipped with sufficient gear to survive the worst weather imaginable. A top-of-the-range GPS makes navigation childs-play. There was a little mist shrouding the top of Ingleborough though by the time we got to the top this had cleared somewhat. Cloud cover kept breaking to reveal surprise views.

We took shelter just off top and had the luxury of a newly brewed hot drink, made from an impressively small stove thing. I'm warming to the idea of this camping thing. If I could do it in the mountains with good equipment in decent weather it may be really good. My memories of roughing it in this country are very much tainted by the all consuming effect eczema had on those experiences. I just wanted to go home.

Other memories- azure blue lake near horton open-cast so incongruous like a wound on the earth, two stiles over walls instead of one (this place must get busy, or are they for overtaking?), A couple working on footpath drainage, wierd peat hags, good to be back in 'limestone country'.
