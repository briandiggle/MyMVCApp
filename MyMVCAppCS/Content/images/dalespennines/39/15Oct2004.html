Great Whernside

15 October 2004

Again the weather came good at just the right time for this walk.

Ross had the reins so I even decided to leave my map behind. After all with compass
,GPS and a mountain leadership course you should be OK. And even if anything should go
wrong, I was confident that there would be enough supplies to oulast a nuclear winter in
Ross's rucksack.

Start of walk was a bit bizarre This Guy-like effigy on top of a 30' high totem poll made of
rusty old barrels greeted us as we entered Kettlewell. There was some kind of urban scarecrow
building contest going on. Or was it to scare outlanders away. The welcome was warmer than
in Horton-in-Ribblesdale. There was no chance this time of starting the Kettlewell riots.

A beautiful clear day with great visability a little wind awaited us. We climbed out of kellewell
on our way towards Hag Dike. Ross's map route choice came slightly into question as he
crossed the stream precariously. I decided to assert my independence and backtracked to
the safety of the path.

Next bit of excitement was the finding of my first geocache. Ross trusted me with his
GPS which I misread and had us wandering about in the wrong direction. I finally found it
at N 54° 09.366 W 002° 00.975, called Which? ditch. It had a scouting theme, as it was
in full view of the Ben Rhydding Scouting centre (empty that day). Could have taken a
woggle or a lump of blue john.

Just as we got to the summit of Great Whernside things turned for the worse as a) we
met the first fellow walkers of the day and b)the cloud rolled in from the nidderdale side.
Ross rushed off to get a glimpse of nidderdale before all disappeared. Actually, the
weather did improve after this, though the clear skies had gone.

Now for a bit of luxury as Madame Chaule gets out her methane stove, kettle, and
sundry accoutrements. Soon were supping hot chocolate ( and claymore whisky in my
case. I declined not to offer Ross any whisky after his comments earlier about its
dehydrating effects!). There was one minor hitch in the process when the methane
stove exploded. Ross put out the conflagration and congratulated himself that he
had followed best advice in planting the bomb a few feet away from us.

We walked along the ridge towards little whernside, which looked as we approached
a nicer shaped mountain than its big brother. Great views of upper nidderdale from
the location of the Nidd Head Spring. We didnt spend much time at the location of the
spring so I can only say that we were in roughly the right place. We then followed the
ridge NE until we picked up the footpath going roughtly SE towards Tor Dike. This
was a traditional dales boggy trudge. At Cam Head we took the track S through
Cam pasture, down to Kettlewell.

Its great to walk with friends. I suppose I feel slightly that walking to me is time when
I can catch up with my best friend and have prescious time with Him - so feel a little
frustrated that there was no chance of this in all the hours away which I had today.
