Sharp Haw and Rough Haw from Skipton

29 November 2006

Skipton Train Station 10:37am -> Sharp Haw 13:22 -> Rough Haw 13:50 -> Skipton Train Station 16:43

Map of GPS Route (Yellow) and Track (Black):

GPX file of track and route

I have two big things to deal with - first and most importantly , thinking about Tanzania and what next steps there are for us. Then the immediate distracting issue of the terribly bad feedback I got from my DI application at work.

At a meeting last week I find that I have been characterised (by someone on the Panel) as having an unhelpful attitude to work and being isolated in the department. I find this initially quite difficult to deal with and today was spent just bringing my anger and sense of injustice to God and trying to battle through to a place of correct perspective and peace that will enable me to return to work and tackle the issue. At the end of the walk I was in a better place, able to face the big questions of the present without the pall of discouragement and confusion that was weighing heavily upon me before.

The day started bright and clear as I wandered up to Skipton Castle. I was directed to the entrance to Skipton Woods - opposite the old Pork Pie Shoppe. The wood itself was closed for renovation work! I decided to enter without the ability to sue if something untoward should happen. Off roaded to the end of the finger of the wood pointing Northwards.

The weather started to turn quite rainy as I left Skipton. But no rain, no rainbow.. and I was treated to a stunning rainbow as I walked North over the golfcourse.

Sharp Haw was a nice little peak. I find it hard to think of airedale as beautiful - must of it is the ex-industrial mess of the Shipley- Bingley etc. But further up its not so bad!
