Simon's Seat from Blubberhouses Moor, 11 Dec 2007

11 Dec 2007

 Blubberhouses Moor A59 -> Rocking Hall -> Brown Bank Head -> Lord's Seat -> Simon's Seat -> Valley of Desolation -> Bolton Bridge

Reminded of my calling to be a father and a husband in mission. So I
need to focus particularly on this - on loving sacrificially Linda and
Emily.

I have been making the mistake of being frustrated when Linda has been
needing strength from me. I have been offended at times that I have
had to use my strength for her rather than for "ministry". I've missed
the mark here -the mark set in Eph 5 where a husband is called to lay
down his life for his wife, for care for her as for his own body.

I saw Jacob returning to the promised land full- with wife, family
and having been blessed and increased by God in his time in exile. Thank
God this is my position now. But remember, this is the blessing which
I must nurture.

I have again missed the mark in being offended when Linda has needed
my strength -difficulty has caused me to make excuses for my own
rebellion. Yet I remembered Lanzarote, when I walked in the life of
Jesus. This is probably the highest note of my walk with God thus far,
and was the time when I "became like Him in His death" - death was at
work in me, life in others. This is the place I must reach for now
and in the future - no outward counterfeit of fruitfulness will do,
but nothing less than to know the power of His resurrection -let this
only satisfy me.

---

This was a glorious day - clear, crisp, visability superb stretching
to the NY moors and hints of far-off Lake District. Perfect conditions -
no wind, cloudless sky.

Often all was quiet. The day started pre-dawn with the 36 to Harrogate,
then the X59, getting off at sunrise on the top of Blubberhouses Moor.
Lunch stop at Rocking Hall was a rare treat - and what incredible views
past Menwith Hill to the Hambleton Hills. Sat on the edge of the left-hand
shelter in the stillness. That until two ladies arrived! I hastily packed
up and left in the direction of Brown Bank Head. This on the map looked
like an objective with the highest point marked. It turned out to be
1Km of picking my way through marshy tussocks. The highest point couldnt
have been anywhere in a 50m square radius!

A quick break in a well appointed shooting hut, then on the high moorland
nearer to Simons Seat, the paths were better
and all was superb, the stillness only broken by the sound of grouse.
Wonderful colours of sky, stone and heather. Visited Lord's seat , and
arrived at Simon's seat. This place is very popular
as a walking destination from Bolton Abbey and seems a bit of a Mecca
for Leeds and Bradford. I was just enjoying the view when the Worst
Case Scenario took place - a jabbering school party approaching the
summit. I had barely time to place an marker roughly on the East
Side of the summit (with no cross on) before the summit was invaded
and occupied by the hordes. It was a large triangular gritstone
marker probably 3 paces eastish in the grass of a narrow gap in the
rock. It is to the left of an inscription in the rock face which
reads "JA 1923 2001"

Soon left the noise behind again and lingered on the high fell
drinking in the sunset before entering the Valley of Desolation.

The walk finished in chasing the X59 into Bolton Bridge.
