Around Fountains Abbey

28th October 2003

Map

Decided on this walk today because it offered some time in forest. I've really been
into the autumn tints this year and have appreciated the beauty of this season
more than ever before. Missed out on a walk in the lakes at the weekend so
this was a bonus.

Started at the car park at fountains bridge with worship blasting away! I felt a
sense of urgency about the future today. It was a day to dream old dreams. A
day for a 'old man' to dream. Headed off into Spring Wood and began praying.
This urgency came on me about our call to the nations. It was a time to take
stock and I felt a need to plan a route to get there.

I entered a clearing in Spa Gill Woods. As I came into this area a real burden
came on me and had to stop, bent and weeping. Oh I want to be there, for
the leaves of the tree are for the healing of the nations. When I look back at
the end of my life I want to know that I have spent the best years of my life
serving the Lord in the nations. Later on I felt that I should pray that I will
be there on my 40th birthday. I want to give at least 20 years. In the clearing
there was a shooting hut/tree house which I climbed into and prayed.
[see photos 1&2]

The next day, the next door neighbour george (aka. teshak) died suddenly of
a heart attack. My cousin stephen had a stroke at the age of 36. I felt the
Lord impress on me the next day the scripture from psalm 90:12 "Teach
us to number our days aright that we may gain a heart of wisdom"

Its a time to really meditate on the promises, dream dreams and take
whatever steps the Lord leads to make those dreams a reality.

I was going to take the footpath from the middle section of Spa Gill
Wood towards Aldfield. But I was too busy praying and found myself
at the main road. A quick section dodging cars before I got to Moor
Lane [photo 3] which took me into the tidy village of Aldfield.

Across the fields after this towards the Obelisk sat comfortable
in fellowship with St Marys Church! Sat on the obelisk to enjoy a
coffee and the first dram of brandy from my hip flask!

Shortly afterwards, on the footpath going towards Studley Roger
I had the first of a number of close encounters with deer and
stags! I was quite daring approaching the stag. Read a sign later
saying that 'for your own safety' do not approach the deer because
it is rutting season!

Took a right turn towards the lake. Devils chimney was a bit eerie.
There were two or three little herds of deer each with a single stag.
These stags stood prominenlty on the steep sides in full view crying
out. This was presumably a variation on "come and have a go if you
think you're hard enough"

Once I got the fountains abbey boundary, the great explorer rose up
in me and I bounded off up the steep forested valley side S of the
footpath in search of a real close encounter with a stag. I realised later
that this was quite a moronic thing to do because I had just passed
the fountains abbey boundary which had an enormous high iron
fence which was clearly there to keep the deer in..

Anyway I was soon glad that I had 'offroaded' as I was just out of
sight when I heard this strange humming sound coming W along
the valley. The humming turned into a hubbub and soon an
enormous party of prattling pensioners filed past underneath me.
I'm sure all the stags had run for cover before they got a chance
to stand en-masse and gawp.

Finally hit the footpath again at the corner of Macjershaw Trough
and headed W, car-ward.

There was an amusing incident at Hill House Farm. I arrived just
behind a farm workers quad-bike and closed the field gate for him.
A dog ran off barking behind him which would otherwise have been
interested in me. Just as I was leaving the farm area it realised there
was an unwelcome guest and decided on a bit of intimidation. I
feigned indifference as it barked at my legs and made it to the
far gate with no chunks missing. I then saw a party of 4 middle
aged walkers 100 yards away standing looking at their maps. I
mischeivous thought occurred to me to warn them off a ravenous
dog and cut short their walk! I had though better of it and was in
the process of walking past when this polite lady asked me whether
the footpath went through the farm. "Yes, through the gate, to the
right of those farm buildings and past the nasty dog" "Oh, right"
she replied.

I went my way. This clearly had the desired effect. I looked back
a couple times and they were still there a couple of minutes later
trying to pluck up sufficient courage to face cerberus. That done
there was still time to take a little detour into the woods to the
W of Robin Hoods Well and have a peek at the abbey through
the trees.
