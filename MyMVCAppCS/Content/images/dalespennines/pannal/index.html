Waited so long for a day which could be roughtly described as a Spring day. But winter dragged on and on. Finally, on the
29th of March I was at work and looked at the weather forecase. It was showing a break in the weather for the next day, so
I took the plunge. By that evening, the forecast had been revised back to its previous position of wind and rain with a few
breaks if you're lucky!

Still, it was good to be out. This walk had been long planned as the holy-grail of the "36 bus series" and really is the last
good walk in new territory that can be done from the 36 route. Walking to and from work since last December has considerably
expanded by range though, so there is scope of longer versions.

Hopped onto the 36 at shortly after 8am

Western section of the walk:

 

Haverah Park was a research site operated by the Physics Department of the University of Leeds. For 20 years it was home to one of the largest extensive air shower arrays in the world with an area of 12 square kilometres. The array was made up of water Cerenkov detectors housed in wooden huts and operated until 1987 when it was switched off.
