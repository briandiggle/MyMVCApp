As I started the walk along Hollybank Lane, I was quickly aware that I am still not through the grieving over no.2 and over disappointment. So I poured out my heart
to the Lord and felt he said

"Come to me in your grief and I will heal and restore you" and was reminded of Psalm 23 "He restores my soul" and Psalm 102 I felt particularly
relevent. How David brought his afflicted heart to God. And I felt God reminded me of His compassion for me

"In their distress He too was distressed" Isaiah 63:7-9

For much of the morning I felt the Holy Spirit was counselling me:

"Don't raise up a heap of stones at a place of failure, returning to it often to remember."

As I was walking along the river on the Nidderdale Way near Birstwith, I passed some Ash trees filled with fruit. And I thought of our marriage - that
we are to bear much fruit in maturing together. I was reminded strongly of the need to serve one-another in love, Ephesians 6.And of the need to
re-focus on the things that really matter - our family.

For emily: We are laying a foundation as master builders.
Also Phil 2:12-13

Therefore, my dear friends, as you have always obeyed—not only in my presence,
but now much more in my absence—continue to work out your salvation with fear and trembling,
13for it is God who works in you to will and to act according to his good purpose

As we continue to build, we will have the joy of seeing her walking in obedience when
we are with her and much more when we are absent.

How do we build? How do we encourage this obedience? As Paul did- 1 Thess 1:4-6

4For we know, brothers loved by God, that he has chosen you, 5because our gospel came to you not
simply with words, but also with power, with the Holy Spirit and with deep conviction.
You know how we lived among you for your sake. 6You became imitators of us and of the Lord;
in spite of severe suffering, you welcomed the message with the joy given by the Holy Spirit.

Other memories from the walk:

The Nidderdale Curlew in mid and late afternoon.

Almost being charged by a herd of cows. One started running at me. Thought if this sets
a trend I'm in trouble! So faced it and made alot of noise - thankfully it backed away.

Strawberries and champagne sorbet at Darley Mill.

Couldnt find the beech tree I planted with Emily in Summerbridge.

Hard going through the heather at Brimham Rocks - boots came loose and caused
blistering which was increasingly a problem later in.

Averaged 5.5Kmh for the last 3 hours of the walk. Once of the final GPS waypoints
was wrong so was giving me an ETA for an extra 2Km - this only served to increase
the late afternoon rush until I was sure the GPS was wrong when getting near to Ripley.
