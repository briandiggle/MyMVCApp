This may have been the longest walk I have ever done.
And I needed the time away. I realised early on in the
walk that I have not for some time been listening to
my heart.

But as I did this now I realised how tough
its been this year. There has been the worry over Linda's
health and the stress that has caused us. The laying down of
my desire for a second child was hard and made more so as I
invested much in the Russian adoption. I realised that
I have given myself little time to work through the real
grieving process. I have known much defeat this year in
the area of impurity as these pressures have come to bear. As I
walked I saw an inward picture of myself walking on, yet
with a grimace of pain, not looking up but looking at my feet.

I remembered turkey in 1993 and feel I am in a similar place to that.
We are preparing increasingly to go into mission, yet I myself feel winded
in the battle and in need of new strength as I face the  beginning of the
biggest spiritual battle of my life – for lives of those in the country of blessing.
I was encouraged to remember how God restored me then and used me
powerfully in Guinea Bissau and asked Him to do it again.

I felt the Lord saying that I need to go at my own pace with Him and not
live out of duty of expectation of what others would have me do in my position.
If anything which comes out of this place of rest will be characterised by grace.

As I had lunch at Woodhouse Bridge the scripture “He who dwells in the
secret place of the most high will rest in the shadow of the Almighty”
came to me. I realised I must discover  this place of rest and strength in
God once again. I need to meet with Him alone. He is the one who will
restore me and enable me to stand strong on the battlefield as the knight
of the Lord, one mighty in battle who brings faith to the hearts of others
also in the fight.

I sang to the Lord “Hide me in the shelter”. This is the place in must find.
This what I now must do.

Other memories from the walk:
Running the gauntlet of some enormous stags eyeing me up in Studley Royal.

Finding the secret door out of Studley Royal at point 6.
No walkers seem all day outside the Ripon Area. Lots of horsey type people.

There was a hunt going on West of Ripon that day. From the high ground near
Owster hill farm I could see the ebb and flow of the dogs and horses. The hunt
trumpet could be heard in the distance numerous times during the day.

Decided to take the detour to Galphay (or Galifray!), then up to Owster Hill at the high point of the day.

Left a marker under a big ol d oak tree at SE26820 70610. See photo. Left in front of
large stone which was in front of the tree. Tree at the intersection of the wall coming
from the N, on the N side of the FP

Lunch at Laver Banks/Woodhouse bridge where God spoke to me about the secret place.

Taking the unmarked FP W at Cow Myers (a very horsy place and perhaps the epicentre of todays hunt )
a old “lad” with a thick geordie accent tried to stop me. I was just relishing the challenge of
instructing him in the  irrefutable facts of the map when one of the “horsy” class informed him
in horsy accent that there was a path. Had to duck under an obstruction though as the path was blocked.

Looking at the ruins of Low House and encouraging more undergrowth to appear inside.

Sneaking up on Azerley Tower, then taking a short cut back to the FP only to have to walk
right past a farmer in his truck. He had no words for me only a look of despair!

Ellington bank FP destroted by horses (there seemed throughout the day to be footpaths
which were being used by horses.
Some had little horsy signs near the FP signs.

Lovely sunset began as I exited Ellington Bank.
Field mist rising off the fields as I returned to Ripon.
