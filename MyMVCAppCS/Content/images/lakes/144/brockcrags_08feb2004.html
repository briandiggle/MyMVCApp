Brock Crags

8th Feb 2004

09:30am Hartsop -> Satura Crag -> Brock Crags 11:10am -> Hartsop 12:10pm

400m ascent, 4.5 km


This was a little more of a battle with the elements.
Snow was lying above about 400m and a strong NW wind
made for a severe wind-chill.

I was the third car in hartsop car park today when I
set off. Didnt see anyone in the vicinity of brock
crags which somewhat restored the lake district in my
mind as a place when solitude can be found.

I was praying about what God had said to me this weekend-
to delight myself in the Lord. I had stumbled last
week becoming very discouraged at finding out (big surprise)
that MVBC is subject to many of the same imperfections
that existed at NewHope. But I am to delight myself in
God, not in church. As I delight myself in God, I can
have vision for a less-than-perfect church and serve
it with joy and with a heart. If the church itself
becomes my delight, hope deferred will soon make the
heart sick.

Walked up towards the familiar filter house. Around
hartsop is probably the area of the lakes I now
know best. Turned to walk directly towards Satura Crag
on the side of Calfgate Gill. Hard work with the wet
snow this. Gray crag looks a fine mountain from this
direction. At the top perched myself in a nook just
below Satura Crag take a nip of whisky and decided to
scale Satura. From the top views north included the
Scottish Southern uplands covered in snow.

Headed then back towards Brock Crags. The ground
was a bit treacherous with bogs hidden by snow cover.
Summit was bitterly cold and I was driven off to take
cover on the south side with fine views of hartsop dodd,
gray crag and high street. Left marker on summit.

I could see some bad weather coming in over loadpot hill
and thought to myself it would be quite exciting to
have a snow shower. I was not dissapointed! It arrived
and I watches as within 10 minutes all the fells were
completely obscured. I could just make out the shape
of gray crag. another 10 minutes and the weather passed,
visability back to 50 miles!

Ambled back to the car. For once I took it easy all the way!
