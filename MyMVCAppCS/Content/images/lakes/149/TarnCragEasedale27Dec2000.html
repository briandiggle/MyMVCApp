Tarn Crag, Easedale

27 December 2000

27.12.2000 From Grasmere via easedale, codale tarnGrasmere -> Easedale Tarn -> Belles Knot -> Codale Tarn (2:30pm) -> Tarn Crag ridge (3:15) -> Tarn Crag - Grasmere

I started off at with the idea of doing High Raise by Pavey Arc but quickly realised that this was too ambitious on one of the shortest days of the year. So decided on Sergeant Man instead. This again proved to be too ambitious as by 2.30pm I had only just reached codale tarn. Walk started in grasmere via the usual route to easedale tarn, then pressed on quickly up to codale tarn. The tip of Belles Knot had been beckoning to me since reaching easedale tarn, its' tip the single point in sunshine in the whole of upper easedale. This is where I chose to take lunch. Decided here that the only realistic target today was Tarn Crag in the hour of full daylight left to me. Headed off for the ridge from Sergeant Man, chasing the falling sun, eager to get out of the gloom of easedale. By 3:15 had reached the ridge and took my first picture of the day as the coniston fells came into view.

1. Looking SW from Sergeant Man - Tarn Crag ridge at 3:20. Blea Rigg in foreground.

2. 2. Same spot, looking towards E with Gibson Knott in the foreground, Dollywaggon Pike on left, then Seat Sandal and Fairfield with St. Sunday Crag behind.

3. 3. Subsidiary summit cairn of Tarn Crag looking towards loughrigg and windermere.
