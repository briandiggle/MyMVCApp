Blea Rigg

28th May 2003

Dungeon Ghyll New hotel Car Park 18:25 -> Blea Rigg 19:40

Lovely clear evening. A good time to venture into the tourist
trap of langdale! I saw very few people on this walk though this
was also due to the unusual route I took via Whitegill Crag.

Pretty steep route this was! Took a right turn at the top which
narrowed to a little chimney at the end. Popping my head over the
top the view S suddenly opens up.

Hard work to find the summit cairn! There were three possibilities.
After having stood on each I decided to place my marker on the
middle one. Weather closed in a little and gave up on Pavey Ark
and headed down via stickle gill

On the way down stickle gill I found a little dell with a secret
waterfall at which point I decided to answer a call of nature. I noticed
then that the tree in front of me had a plaque. It read "Sarah Wilcock"

Back down to the New Dungeon Ghyll hotel I read go myself a pint
and sat outside looking across to Lingmoor Fell reading scripture.

I'm at a real point in my christian life where God has revealed to me
a "path drenched with cream" and yet to walk in it requires great
cost. I want to get there as I know this represents the "rocketing"
spiritually which Yinka prophesied. Yet I see also my old life does
not want to die. Some areas I seem to have the ability to put to
death easily and yet other I cannot. Rees Howells had the revelation
that "self cannot give up self" and saw how God in His grace "pulled
him through" to that higher life. I am now praying that God will
"pull me through" also. Oh God lead me into a life of great
surrender. Help, Holy Spirit! This battle results in times of great
dismay at times. I think most christians only experience the cross as
the place of their cleansing. Here they stay in a life which lacks
power and overcoming. I desire to press on to experience the cross
as my deliverance not from sin only, but the nature of sin, from self
and from my self-will.God help me not to give up!
