High Hartsop Dodd

From Sykeside campsite

22 September 2002

Only 2.5 hours to fit in a walk, so <2000 ft. After getting stuck
behind a couch going over kirkstone pass, only had about 1hr 45 mins
daylight to climb high hartsop dodd. Took the footpath next to the inn
at the bottom of the pass, and had to endure walking through a crowded
campsite.
Soon was off the track heading towards the sharp point of high hartsop
dodd as it juts out towards patterdale. It was a lovely late september
evening, absolutely clear, quiet and calm. Shame I was on a tight
timescale really.

On the way up, two markers are the first and second walls which
are spaced equally. Its a nasty climb and I was very gald to come out
onto the summit plateau. But where was the summit. Two indistinct
humps were candidates. The map gave no clues, so had to turn to
wainwright for the definitive answer of something like "it is
generally agreed that the first rise after reaching the plateau
is the summit". Good thing too, as the second one may have been
too far! The summit was a very small pile of stones, but my
attension was drawn to the view of dovedale which was quite awesome
with the sun setting over Hart crag.


Time for a quick time with the Lord in which I was encouraged
in the major battle I'm in at the moment.

Then, as emily would say after having climbed onto my knees, 'Down'.

Timimg was about 50 mins up, 35 mins down.
