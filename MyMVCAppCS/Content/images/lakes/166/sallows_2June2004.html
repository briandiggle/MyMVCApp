Sallows

2 June 2004

From Kentmere church (7:50pm) via Whiteside End, Sallows(9:10pm), Garburn pass, Kentmere Ch (10:20pm)

7km, 366m ascent.

Didnt expect to see anyone, and was right!

A very calm evening, no wind at all. Just a little dull with high cloud.

Took a long way round for the ascent, heading round whiteside end looking for the old mining track. I finally found it though it was hardly discernable at times and really took me off track into the glacial morraine area between sallows and sour howes. I was rewarded with a view of some deer on the horizon (fairly close) and the scripture "he makes my feet like the feet of a deer- he ables me to walk on my high places"

Nearing the location of the old mine I came across a badger about 20 feet away scuttling to find its hole.Once I had found the mining track a poor guide I decided to rely on a guess that striking due N would take me to the summit. I was out by only 20 feet!

It was very quiet on the summit and I had time to spare and prayed that God would enable me to really walk in my high places. Left a marker and was down by 10:20 still just light.
