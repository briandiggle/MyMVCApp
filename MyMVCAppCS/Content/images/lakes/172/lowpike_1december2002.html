Low Pike

Sunday 1st December 2002

Ambleside (Lesketh How) 2:10pm - High Sweden Bridge - Low Pike 3:00pm - Low Sweden Bridge - Ambleside (Lesketh How) 3:50pm

Description
Up in kendal for a long weekend. According to the forecast
for this weekend, several new lakes were likely to form.
I'd almost given up hope of the rain stopping when there
was an unscheduled break midday on sunday.
Linda was nice about me disappearing and by 2:10 I was
parked up at Lesketh How just past Ambleside (no room at
the inn)

It was a lovely early winter scene which developed as I
climbed out of ambleside towards High Sweden Bridge. The
sun soon disappeared but the lack of rain seemed miraculous
enough!

I followed Scandale Beck up through a section of ancient
forest hugging its' gorge. Once High Sweden bridge was
crossed I felt like I was 'on the fells'. Had a good prayer
time in the early part of the walk before time-pressure
cut in. Feel like I'm back on track in terms of intercession
since during the major battle of Aug-Nov 2002 saw me get out
of the habit.

After taking the first part of the walk at a easy pace, realised
I'd have to push it to get to my objective. This is where my
delusions began about the identity of Low Pike. I assumed it
was the obvious peak above me as AW descrived it as the 'first
prominent peak seen from the streets of ambleside'. In fact
I was struggling to climb High Pike with only 1hour ascent
time from Lesketh How!

The time factor became more focused by a man coming
down with his two daughters. He clearly thought I was off my
rocker and didnt seem relieved that I was only ascending Low
Pike! His daughter wished me 'good luck' as we parted! After
this I did a bit of fell running through the marshy hollows.

So I reached a peak at 3pm assuming it to be high brock crags.
Looked at the watch and was getting really disappointed that
I just didnt quite have the time to reach Low Pike.
Began to come to my senses when looking at the map and seeing
the pattern of the stone walls on the red screes side of scandale
beck seemed to indicate that I was near Low Pike. Turned to AW
and the drawing of High Pike from Low pike was a clincher!

After a quick stop on the summit to admire my twice unexpected
acheivement, turned for home. First spots of rain hit my face
like bullets as I did so. Declared to caradhras that I had
triumphed and she could throw whatever she desired at me in her
anger.

Excitement on the way down was swinging off a branch to negociate
the last part of High Brock crags (near the Rock Step). Back at
the car in the dark for 3:50pm
