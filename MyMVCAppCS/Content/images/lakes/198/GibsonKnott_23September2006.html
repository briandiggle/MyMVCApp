Grasmere -> Helm Crag -> Gibson Knott -> Greenburn -> Grasmere

The actual walk was more of a D!

I was expecting to come into this walk greatly encouraged. The door to the nations is opening. We are about to arrange a
visit to YWAM Tanzania in November. Dreams long held seem about to be fulfilled.

Then we share all this with Andy Hobbs. His response is less than encouraging. Instead of affirming our calling and
seeking to support and back us, we find he has picked up along the way a quite negative impression of YWAM. "Have
you considered any other organisations?" He then goes on to push BMS. The meeting is largely taken up with
us defending YWAM unsucessfully. Afterwards I was really discouraged. Angry that he had no prepared or prayed
at all. He had squeezed in another meeting prior to ours and arrived late. Shades of that "meeting" about Gipton with
John Sloan. I even found myself saying "you're a very busy man" at the end as the similarity of the meetings was so
strong.

I prayed that Isaiah 55 will be fulfilled for us:

You will go out in joy
       and be led forth in peace;
       the mountains and hills
       will burst into song before you,
       and all the trees of the field
       will clap their hands.

I prayed that MVBC and Andy will send us out with conviction, joy and peace - even with celebration. That
they will commission us in faith and confidence.

This walk was something similar to that. All week lovely weather had been forecast. Only just before the walk
fog was mentioned as forming in the night, then clearing. So I did the walk and had to cut it short due to the
poor conditions - cloud at 1700'. Upon descent to greenburn, the weather cleared up almost immediately as if in
mockery.

The sense of accomplishment was really due to having set the record for the earliest wainwright ascent at 8:14am on
Helm Crag. This after setting off from Kendal at 6am.

The original plan for the day was in fact Helvellyn via striding edge, then back via Swirral Edge and Catstycam. I turned
right at Windermere on the Kirkstone Pass road. First glimpse of the Kirkstone Pass fells showed the mist right down.
I turned round and headed for Grasmere.
