Black Fell

22/4/2003

Skelwith Bridge -> Black Fell -> Skelwith Bridge


 

This day started out sounding like a walking day on a methodist
guild holiday! The A party were heading for Dove Crag via Dovedale.
B party going up Blea Rigg. The pathetic C party were scaling Black
Crag.
I joined the C party after seeing the low cloud level. Fancied a walk
through some forest round Skelwith Bridge.

Parked at the Jorysz hotel a.k.a. Skelwith Bridge hotel. This walk was
a game of two halves. First half was a leisurely (prayerful) walk
skirting the edge of Black crag on two sides. The second half was
a frenzied attempt to get back to the car by 12:30. Not much prayer
took place on the second half but it was fun.

First Half: 09:45 -11:10
Over skelwith bridge. Nursed throught the only finding of the footpath
towards Colwith force (the first footpath sign said "If you're looking
for the footpath to Colwith Force its 100 yards further up the road"
in about that many words!)
Crossed over the minor road to Elterwater and enjoyed walking through
the forest towards the charmingly named "Little Fell" - dappled sun
on brand new bright green beech leaves. Down the lane SE towards the
coniston road. It was about this time that I realised I didnt have
much time to complete the walk. I was faced with the definate prospect
of failing to acheive the 1026' of Black Crag! The rest was a Yomp...

Second Half: 11:10
No time to look at the map. Navigation mistake soon followed. took the
road to Oxen Fell at the junction. Took the next turn correctly and
was soon yomping up towards the Iron Keld plantation. I split the
second half into two 40 minute sections with the summit of Black crag
in the middle at 11:50. By the time I got into the Iron Keld plantation
I realised this wasnt on but was quite enjoying the speed. Left a
couple of overweight middle-aged walkers in my wake. Reached the summit
huffing and puffing at 12:00 and placed a marker.
Now the only way to get to the car for 12:30 was the direct route N
over the feel. Ignored the sign saying access was only to a viewpoint
N and off I went. Some fell running was required while stuffing sandwiches
in my face. Tried to keep N but the will of the mountain 'turned me
ever Westwards' and I found myself at barbed wire fence at the squiggly
start of the C road at the western end of park fell. Sprinted the final
100M in a time of 9:89 seconds to reach the car at 12:45
