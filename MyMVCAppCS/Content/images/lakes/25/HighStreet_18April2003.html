High Street

18 April 2003

Haweswater CP 11:03 -> East ridge mardale ill bell (from Small Water) -> Mardale Ill Bell 12:58pm -> High Street 1:30pm -> Kidsty Pike 1:58pm -> Haweswater CP 15:35pm

Great conditions for this walk in mid-may! Started off in shorts
and tea shirt. The only downside was that it was quite hazy so could
only see mid-distance. Sunshine all day.

Thought on good-friday in these conditions that I would have to
choose an out-of-the-way place to avoid the crowds. Half the population of
the UK had the same idea. Haweswater car park holds about 15 cars. When
I arrived at 11am cars were parked back from there for about 600M! People
were arriving all the time. Ah well I decided to accept the crowds and
started towards Small water. Wasnt easy to pray trying to keep people
ahead and behind out of ear-shot!

By the time I reached Small water I was longing to 'off-road'. There
were walkers behind every rock and crevice. Decided to avoid the
motorway to Nan Bield Pass and go up the East ridge of Mardale Ill Bell.
As I struck off on my own there was the opportunity to wave at one
walker sidling off behind a large boulder for an obvious purpose. He
waved back!

At last I was able to enjoy the fell and talk to the Lord about wanting
to live in the Spirit. "In his presence are pleasures forever more and
fullness of joy"- nothing the world offers compares.

Had lunch halfway up the E ridge where I found a triangular stone and
decided on the format of my "marker": Stand at the E side of the cairn,
Walk 5 paces E, place triangular stone here pointing E, having marked
underside of the stone with a cross.

Struck up the virtually non-existent E ridge to hit the summit exactly.
Surprisingly I had it to myself (for a few minutes) and placed my first
marker!

Now I began the time-trial: Could I get to kidsty pike and be back at
the car at 3:15? Set off at top-speed towards High Street. In 20 mins
I was there. My dodgy hip wasnt getting in the way much. Just time to
leave a marker and move on my way to Kidsty Pike. Scenery around the
straits of Riggindale was awesome. Reached Kidsty Pike in 25 mins. View
of high street fells was great from the summit. I dont find summits are
great places to linger when there is a crowd :-(

Off on another romp again taking a short cut from the crowds to the S
of Kidsty Howes. Detoured round the Rigg finally and back for 3:35pm
