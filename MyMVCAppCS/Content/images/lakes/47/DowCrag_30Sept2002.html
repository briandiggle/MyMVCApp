Dow Crag

30th September 2002

Quite jaded today at the end of a cold, so found this walk a
bit of a slog. Much of it was in fog which was supposed to clear.

On the way along coniston kept passing old giffers on endfields
looking like wallce in leathers. There was some kind of meet around
the pub at torver. Some of the guys looked ancient, in their 80's,
red faced, thick glasses, wearing biggles caps.

Escaped the din opposite the pub. Passed this over-talkative old
lady picking blackberries along the footpath. Chose to keep away
from the walna scar highway for as long as possible.

Trudged up rather slowly, mist was probably about 1500' at most.
A spurt of energy took me to brown pike. On I went and came across
a couple eating sandwiches on the next summit and got it into my
head this was the summit of dow crag. Even took a picture! So was
rather surprised to find the ground rising again on the other
side of this summit (buck pike)

Headed back bia Goats water which was rather eerie. Echoing voices of
rockclimber could he heard in the clouds on the other side of the water.

And if it didnt clear up as soon as I was down...
