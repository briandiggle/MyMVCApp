Harter Fell

10th February 2003

Longsleddale Sadgill 09:05 -> Gatescarth Pass -> Adam Seat -> Harter Fell 11:20 ->
Kentmere Pike 12:10 -> Shipman Knotts 13:15 -> Sadgill 14:25

Decided that it was high time I got to know the mountains and valleys closest to kendal better. Started the day with a plan A and plan B. Plan A was to  climb Kentmere Pike via Steel Rigg and then loop round to Gatescarth pass taking Harter Fell on the way. Plan B was Sallows and Sour Howes if the weather was bad.

By the time the final decision was made I was onto about plan G! Parked at Sad Gill in sunshine but was dissappointed to see that there was mist at about 2000' at the head of Longsleddale. I was glad to get anything in today really with worries about my left eye being up to it. But it greatly improved on Sunday. A pulled muscle in my right leg was nearly right but there was a doubt over how it would stand up to a big walk. In the event it wasnt a problem.

Started off up Sad Gill talking to the Lord about the future. A break in the pressure of work has given me time to think more about our long term plans. It has though been difficult so far this year to keep focused and free from fear as worries about the eye have persisted. It is a real  battle against fear. It's been encouraging to see how Linda has seen a real breakthrough in this area recently and has supported me to overcome the giant of fear in my own life.

She also felt God say recently that promises about missions place us in the shoes of Abraham who was strengthened in faith and gave glory to God because he believed the promises even though he knew his body was as good as dead. That is our position - in the natural going as a family into mission is so out of the pattern of our lives right now. But we must believe and encourage ourselves in the promises also. This confirmed just what God had said to me after emily arrived. I am living now in the fulfillment of all Gods promises of being married and being a Father. I inherited those promises by faith. Now I must do the same with the second great faith challenge of my life - our calling to mission.

Today became an example of the life of walking by faith and an encouragement that to those who trust, God is faithful to his promises. That seasons change quickly, like dry river beds in the Negev suddenly filled with water.

Walking up Backbarrow Crag I thought I remembered reading in Wainwright that the Steel Rigg route should not be attempted in mist. A quick check confirmed this. Plan C was then hatched - Tarn Crag and Grey Crag on the other side of Longsleddale were as yet clear of mist. Climb them starting a Brownhowe Bottom.

As I climbed towards this I began thinking that with all the time I had, more could be accomplished. So Plan D became Plan C plus branstree, going E from the head of Gatescarth pass.

I was walking along the gatescarth pass byway above Wren Gill when the  weather suddenly closed in. In the space of 5 minutes, from being able to see all of the East side of Longsleddale and the enclosed valley, I was in mist and the only familiar feature was the byway! As wainwright warned  against Tarn Crag in mist, plans C and D were dead! Enter plans E,F and G...

Plan E was to follow the line of the fence E from the head of Gatescarth Pass to Branstree and retrace my steps. Plan F was to follow the fence W of this point taking in Adam Seat and Harter Fell then retracing steps. Plan G was plan F but continue after Harter Fell along the fence and take in Kentmere Pike and Shipman Knotts.

I arrived at the pass. The wind was whistling past with a fearful windchill. I took a deep breath and headed W towards Adam Seat. This is where I began to walk by faith! The conditions were nasty. Ice had formed in the direction of the wind on any obstacle in its path. There were some wierd formations on the wire fence and tall grass. I soon had to protect myself as much as  possible from the wind with the kagool leaving just a small gap through  which to see.

I relied for peace of mind on the compass and map, referring to them  often. Arriving at what I thought was adams seat I was saw on the map  the symbol BS. Had some fun opening up the map to find the meaning of this symbol - Boundary Stone. Sure enough there was an ancient erect stone on which was carved the symbol 'L'. Continued up towards Harter Fell passing through some snow fields. Came across the first wierd cairn of Harter Fell with its nightmarish quality. Pressed on towards Harter Fell encouraged by another correctly located Boundary Stone, this time inscribed with an 'L'

After stopping briefly at the summit of Harter Fell I decided to go for Plan G! It was a long walk along the top towards what I knew should be kentmere pike. I had soon lost my sense of direction, relying on the compass completely, checking regularly that I was on track by confirming that the fence was swinging round towards a SE direction. Still after so long relying entirely on the map and compass, doubts start to creep in. Fear grows even though you're sure that , according to the map, you're right on course. I was a relieved when the ground began to swell again, presumably towards Kentmere Pike summit. The cairn and OS column confirmed that I was still OK.

Just before reaching the summit I had a short sense that the gloom had  brightened a little. I stopped dead and wheeled round to where I thought the light was and thought I could detect something. This happended a few times.

Settled down to lunch on Kentmere Pike. It happened again - a chink of light in the howling gloom! Then a definite white path came and went. Suddenly a bright expanse of sky appeared...I jumped up. In a few moments an awesome vista opened up - a caught a glimpse of a mountain ridge on the other side of the wall and began to shout for joy! The mist then broke into ribbons and all was revealed. It happened so fast...

It took me a while to orientate myself. What I initially thought was ullswater or thirlmere I realised was Windermere. The range I first saw was the Western side of Kentemere - Yoke, Ill Bell and Froswick.

Finally I set off towards Shipman Knotts in sunshine. Took a quick detour to Goat scat to inspect it after its foreboding display this morning. On shipman Knotts I decided to build a better cairn. Hopefully cleared up a bit of confusion by removing a large stone from the southern most grassy knoll and placing it on the middle (highest) one.

Back down to Sadgill via Sadgill wood where I lingered for a while before crossing the bridge over the Sprint. To my delight as I did this, the first drops of rain began to fall!

Footnote 8 November 2009:
We are moving onto lifes plan C after seeing the  "second great faith challenge of my life - our calling to mission" not work out due to the mess of WEC Senegal. Might life's journey end up at Plan G?
