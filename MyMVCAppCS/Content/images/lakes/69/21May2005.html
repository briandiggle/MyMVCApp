Seat Sandal

21 May 2005

Mill Bridge, Grasmere 11:50 -> Seat Sandal 13:45 -> Grizedale Tarn 14:15 -> Glenridding 16:15

Up in kendal for a weekend with emily I was planning
when I could squeeze a walk in but Mum second guessed
and had one of her "good ideas"- why not walk "through
the gap" and be picked up in Glenridding. I bit her hand
off but planned to make a slight diversion to Seat Sandal.
As I was set down at Mill Bridge, a quick negociation gave
me an extra half-hour and I had four hours and ten minutes
to do the walk instead of the 3.5 hours bernard takes.

So off I went straight up the side of Seat Sandal I have
looked at dozens of times from grasmere. The weather was
a treat as well, with heavy showers predicted, there was
no mist and only a few drops of rain.

I needed this break to get thinking clearly again. There
is so much (too much) pressure on at home and I have
really felt the need of a break for some time. Yet I'm
aware that 4 hours isnt really enough. Talking to Pete
yesterday- he has a yearly retreat to the Lakes for 3/4
days and on the first day he often finds his head is
still buzzing with all the life of home. I think I
was in that place really still at the end of the walk -
refreshed but not really fully back on track.

I felt what God was emphasising was that I must press
in will all energy to "lay hold of that for which Christ
Jesus laid hold of me". I came to Him first with the
heart cry "I just want to be free" and His response was
"lay hold of me and you will be free indeed". There
was a burden to press in to experience the "glorious
liberation of the children of God". It is not Gods
heart that I merely "control the flesh" but that I
"put on Christ". I am in a battle of deliverance at
present and must press through.

I met a total of two people on Seat Sandal and am
convinced that the sparcity of people was partly
due to today Cup Final! I had the summit all to
myself and placed the marker (Note 5 paces from
estimated centre of cairn) before descending
steeply to grizedale tarn where I joined the
relative A road of traffic. Though in truth there
were still few people about it was enough to make
me seek refuge by walking down grizedale on the
opposite side to the main path.

By the time I reached the place where a path crosses
over the stream I was "up against the clock" and
yomped the last 45 minutes. Raced up to lanty's
tarn it coming into view at exactly 4 o'clock
(the agreed meeting time).

The last treat of the day was walking through the
sea of bluebells in flower on the fell side taking
their turn of prominence before the bracken overshadowed
them for the rest of the summer.

Joined a Pendlebury family tradition of a pot of tea
at the "Greystones Cafe" in Glenridding. It was great
to walk down the final stretch into Glenridding and
see emily in the distance walking along the stream
wall outside Greystones.
