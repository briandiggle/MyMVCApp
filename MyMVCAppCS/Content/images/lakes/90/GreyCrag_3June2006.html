Gray Crag

3 June 2002

2 hours 25 mins from Hartsop

As the rain had beaten the ground for the previous two days almost non-stop, I was giving up hope of climbing anything on this trip to kendal. Suddenly the rain stopped, and by the time I reached hartsop car park there was hardly a cloud to be seen.

I was looking for solitude today. Not loneliness, but being alone with God revelling in His awesome creation. And I chose the right place- everyone seemed to be on the main road to high street, which I quickly turned off and struck straight up the summit ridge. Views back to ullswater and surroundings were great, especially as I now feel like I know this part of the lakes pretty well.

I had the summit plateau to myself. I raised my hands to God at the summit as I was praying. Theres so much I want to learn about the surrendered life, walking in the footsteps of the precious Holy Spirit. But how quick I am to stumble. Teach me to do your will O God, Let your good Spirit lead me on level ground.

This time I made sure that I took time to enjoy the walk more and experience the beauty around me, rather than living too fast in the pursuit of acheivement (could I have got to threshwaite mouth?!)

Went down a slightly different way walking off right towards the end of hayeswater, but here I struck the crowds so didnt linger there on my way down. Back for emilys tea!
