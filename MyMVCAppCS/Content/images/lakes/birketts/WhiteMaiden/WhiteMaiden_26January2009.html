White Maiden ring from Seathwaite Lodge, Seathwaite, Dunnerdale 

26 January 2009

Seathwaite -> Walna Scar -> White Maiden -> White Pike -> Pikes -> Caw -> Seathwaite

Total Time:  6 hours 16 mins
Total Distance: 11.3Km
Average Speed:  1.8Kmh

Day started after a good English breakfast at the tradition start time of 9:30 ish. Bright sunshine in
Dunnerdale was not to last as a bank of cloud  advanced visiblly from the SW turning light blue into
dull grey.

By the time the final ascent of the Walna Scar pass was being made, the scene resembled more
a mountaineeing expedition as the track led upwards into a dull grey snowfield. At the pass, the
traffic was met briefly coming up from Coniston, but turning away to Walna Scar summit I was
largely alone again except for a keen bagger or two adding a couple of extras to their days haul
on the main Coniston Massif.

Here on top in the snow surrounding Walna Scar summit, though the scene was dull, all was quiet.
I enjoyed a snack and coffee here in a calm unexpected for a fell top in January.

Shortly though after this break, the day turned into more of a mountaineering challenge as mist
descended, obscuring the route from  White Maiden to White Pike. And I was thrown back
onto my raw skills, my GPS not having the waypoints for these two. I first chose to follow the
snow prints of the bagger but realised after a while that he may be heading first for White Pike.
Then trusting to my compass skills I navigated well from White Maiden using the wall as an 
excellent guide.

The trickiest section of the walk was now ahead- the section from White Pike to Pikes, a
route not found in any guidebook. I chose a route almost due south to avoid what looked
on the map to be some buttresses to the SW of White Pike's summit. I chose this route
well and wove my way down though a steep trough with these buttresses appearing to
my right. Going was not easy due to the melting snow.

Made quick progress to Pikes, which was probably my favourite peak of the day. An
unfrequented place like this gives an impression that today Pikes belonged to me - the
hill waited patiently for my arrival in the afternoon and then returned not solitude on my
departure. On leaving the top though I was sorely tempted by the mass of Caw dominating the view.
Caw had avoided my grasp once yesterday, just out of range of the day's expedition.
I was faced with the saw goading mountain today. Was there enough daylight left
to conquer it. After enjoying my rest on Pikes I was ready for a dash up caw and 28 minutes
after leaving Pikes I was standing exultant on Caw, who nearly got her revenge with her
slippery summit rocks. The GPS did well to come out unharmed from its 10 foot plunge off
the summit rocks onto the grass below!

There was now only time for a direct descent off Caw towards Seathwaite before the light failed.
