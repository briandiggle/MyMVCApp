Ashstead Fell from Hucks Bridge

28 December 2009

Average Speed: 1.2 km/h
Maximum Speed: 8.6 km/h
Minimum Speed: 0.0 km/h
Total Time: 03:01:29
Cartographic Length = 3.647 km

Conditions: 8 inches of snow was left from the heavy falls before Christmas. The top layer was frozen but was normally unable to support my weight. No wind, excellent visability.

Five weeks ago I braved the torrential rain to climb the first peak on the Northern side of the Borrowdale valley. It was the worst floods in living memory taking place just after we arrived back from Senegal. Then I was fresh from the escape out of that impossible situation.

Today, about a month later,  I walked the first peak on the Southern side of the valley in conditions caused by the worst snowfall in the lakes for at least ten years. I was aware of how much I have travelled internally since then, how the Lord has brought me along and spoken to me. I came to this walk with a much clearer and more confident mental attitude, feeling ready for the next challenge - to make a new start in Leeds at the beginning of a new era of my life. The post-mission phase where dreams of serving the Lord overseas are not dead by definitively on the altar waiting for God to provide a ram. Dreams now must be (perhaps as they should always have been) about the person I want to be in God rather than the things I want to do for God. There is still though a hunger to serve him meaningfully and extend His Kingdom which right now I dont know what to do with.

Though I am faced imminently with return to all that is familiar, this walk was anything but. I have never walked in such alpine-esque conditions. Thick snow,clear air, long vistas. It was a new experience of winter mountaineering that has whetted my appetite for more!

The first 10 minutes of the walk were rather irritating as I was trying to get to grips with the use of crampons. The crampons first came off as I was crossing the A6, after which I realised that the strap needs feeding not just round but underneath. They came off again shortly after leaving the road when I was passed by two smug walkers without crampons who had just finished the full Borrowdale round! Anyway on reflection it was worthwhile wearing them as, though they were cumbersome, they did ensure a good grip at all times.
