Humphrey Head

7th December 2003
Kents Bank railway station - Humphrey head - Abbot hall tower - Kents bank railway station

My legs were not willing to undergo a repeat of yesterdays torture so I was looking for an easy option without any real ascent! Humphrey head is I think the lowest peak in the 'outlying fells of lakeland'. It surrounds have many memories. The walk started with blasting out 'Oh God you are my God and I will ever praise you'. A song which speaks to me now of promises fulfilled and hope for the promises which remain. Quick look at the 'beach' across the track. Scenes of battles against the tide when staying at abbot hall, it is now grass and mud. Most of the rock have been swallowed up. How faithful God has been to bring to me this place now - a husband and father!

Set out along the headland took a coffee round the corner from Kirkhead End ont he railway embankment. Read againt the story of how God took Abram out to show him the night sky. It struck me that He was not only asking Abram to count the stars, but also to consider His awesome power, the glory of his creation. And as Abram consider this, he believed that the One who made the universe by his power and great understanding could also fulfill his promise to Abraham to give him an heir from his own body. I also think of the promises upon my life now and am greatly encouraged.

My meditation was rudely interrupted on the path past Wyke farm. The sun was directly ahead dazzling my eyes. The ground seemed untrustworthy. One step ahead and to the right and I would be in the shade of Wyke Farmhouse and be able to see the ground ahead. That was one step too far! In an instant I had disappeared to my knees. I'm thankful now it was not deeper. I grabbed the side and pulled myself out. I was covered in some unholy mixture of mud and manure! Sat down for another coffee and decided to soldier on.

Soon found myself at Humphrey Head with great sea-scapes. Decided for a spot of 'off-roading' on the way back. Struck off directly for the tower on the hill above Abbot Hall. I remember once doing the walk legally from Abbot Hall but now the path seems to have fallen into disrepair and the tower little frequented. Also found what seemed like a makeshift tree-house on top. On the way down I saw a deer before walking through the grounds below the house. Couldnt see the putting green, scene of many great victories over old ladies. Past the knarled old tree at the end of the main lawn and resisted the temptation to climb.
