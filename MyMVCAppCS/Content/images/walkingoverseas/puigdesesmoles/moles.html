Puig de Ses Moles, 736m

(exact same height as Seat Sandal, the previous walk)

23rd June 2005

Map showing GPS route in Pink and actual track in black.

Zooming in on the track section around Coll Cuiro. My attempt to scale the wall to Caragoler can be seen and the excursion to Moles

The plan was
to head for the pass of Coll Cuiro and then make an assault on
the summit 1000' above from there. Due to the poor nature of the
map (contours at 50m intervals) I wasn't able to tell if a route
was possible from the pass in advance. The only hint I had
was a walk summarised on a spanish web site which suggested that
there was a route which someone had taken before.

Woke up at 6:30 and looking outside I was disappointed (?) to
discover a clear sky. It was already warm outside. I found out
the next day talking to the owner of a local vineyard that this
had been the hottest day of the year so far at 34.8 degrees

Arrived at the tiny parking space at Mosset at 7:30 (room for
two cars max), donned my "pico redondo" hat and set off walking
towards Femenia Nou through pine forest on a decent track.
Lovely and Quiet except for a shepherd somewhere in the valley
shouting at his flock.

From Femenia Nou the views down into pollensa were panoramic.
An idlyllic scene - the farmhouse perched on a terrace of the
tramantuna mountain rage overlooking most of North-West mallorca.
Pico de Maria/Santuari near pollensa was there and somewhere
just behind that- our finca and pigs.

Approaching Femenia Vell the way was barred by an unchained
(sleeping) dog. First opportunity for off-roading turned out
to be a clever short-cut. The dog never woke up from its
slumber! On past femenia vell I came to a terrace on the
hillside on the right of the path and went to explore and
take a break. From here Puig Massat and Puig Gros de Ternelles
were very evident. The land towards the sea looked wild and
pristine.

The track up towards Coll Cuiro afforded good progress so that
by 9.00 I was nearly at the Coll. Thinking I was making
wonderful progress, I decided to add another peak which was
beckoning to the right of the pass - Puig de Ses Moles 736m.
This turned out to be an hour-long diversion as the peak I
was aiming for was a sham - the real summit was set back
a considerable way.

At the summit, great views of the North West. Formentor was
behind the bulk of Ternelles. Puig Tomir loomed to the South
(glad I didnt attempt this). I found what looked like a
nativity scene in one of those old chemists medicine bottles.
It had decayed somewhat but the crib and other extras were there.
Then I noticed a 35mm film canister and thought that I had
found a micro-cache by chance. It turned out to be a personal
micro-cache left by someone in Feb 1997. 8 years later it was
still in great nick.

Left a marker without a mark. The pen knife which would have
been helpful at this point caused alot of interest at Palma airport
two days later... The marker was left 5 paces east-ish from the
summit cairn directly on the line to the summit of
P. de Maria/Santuari near Pollensa town.

Back to the pass and the next task was to find a way up
onto the summit plateau. The were two mountain shelves I had
to scale. From a distance, the first shelf looked to have a
weakness to the right. The second shelf looked to have a
somewhat more dubious weakness to the left. As to whether
a path exists on top of the first shelf leading to the
second weakness was anyones guess.

The first one proved to be easy - just a single five foot
section of rock climbing with handholds. And there turned
out to be an easy path to the second weakness. However
as I approached the second weakness, it became increasingly
evident that this was no push-over! It was a ten-foot section
of sheer vertical rock with even as slight overhang. I gave
it a go - but this was REAL rock-climbing! The handholds
required finger-strength. The footholds were more toe-holds.
I clung on half way up, wondering what to do. A final 'go'
persuaded me to give it up. I leaned and stretched forward,
grabbing hold of a better handhold, placing myself in a
more 'committed' position. Then the handhold moved! I
retreated, my heart pumping! After this I was more than
happy to settle for Puig De Ses Moles.

There was an area of easy cultivated ground round the corner
frm the Coll Cuiro which lulled me into thinking this was
going to be easy. The next 2.5 hours were anything but-
fighting my way through a tangled mass of rocks and thick
vegetation. The area between Coll Cuiro and Coll des Pinetans
was a morass of sharp rocks and think vegetation. By the end
of the walk my legs had numerous lacerations.

The going became a little easier from Coll Des Pinetans as
the path was cairned. Walking now was a case of looking for
some stones poking out above the mass of rocks and vegetation
which appeared to be man-made - and heading for it. Then the
next..

The area felt like the "dark side of the moon". Add to this
the now stifling heat, no shade and lack of any wind. I got through
nearly 3 litres of water. The cooler pine forest of the other
side would have been welcome.

As I approached Coll des Ases and found the first Pine tree
for about three hours. This represented to me a return to civilisation.
This really was the closest I have been to a real wilderness in
my life. I felt an edge of anxiety at the starkness of the
terrain which took the edge of my usual carefree enjoyment of
"the fells". Thus I didnt revel in the experience as I had done
in Lanzarote. Yet I know that my heart was not alive as it had
been there. The book I'm reading at present talks about the need
for men to rediscover their hearts, and to do that you need to
head for the wilderness. I think there needs to be for me a certain
level of confidence and familiarity with the experience and environment
in order to allow mental space for "deeper thinking". And probably
alot more time. Here there was also the pressing need to get back on
time or make Linda fearful.

Coll des Ases has a Pine tree at its summit. Here I sat down facing
back inland and felt that I had "come out of radio silence". Now
I relaxed and could pray again. Before this there was no room in
my head for thoughts higher than "where is the next pile of stones?"
Soon I was greeted with another sure sign of mans nearness - the
gentle clanging of a "goat bell"

The highlight of the remaining time was the picturesque farm of
Mossa with its wheat fields literally white for harvest, and the
knarled ancient olive-trees dotted about in the fields like old
ents walking.

Finished off with a nasty bit of "off-roading" cutting off a
corner to the closed section to mossa farm.
